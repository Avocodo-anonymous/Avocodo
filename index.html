Avocodo: Generative Adversarial Network for Artifact-free Vocoder


Abstract

Neural vocoders based on the generative adversarial neural network (GAN) have been widely used due to their fast inference speed and lightweight networks while generating high-quality speech waveforms. Since the perceptually important speech components are primarily concentrated in the low-frequency band, most GAN-based vocoders perform multi-scale analysis that evaluates downsampled speech waveforms. This multi-scale analysis helps the generator improve speech intelligibility. However, in preliminary experiments, we observed that the multi-scale analysis which focuses on the low-frequency band causes unintended artifacts, e.g., aliasing and imaging artifacts, and these artifacts degrade the synthesized speech waveform quality. Therefore, in this paper, we investigate the relationship between these artifacts and GAN-based vocoders and propose a GAN-based vocoder, called Avocodo, that allows the synthesis of high-fidelity speech with reduced artifacts. We introduce two kinds of discriminators to evaluate speech waveforms in various perspectives: a collaborative multi-band discriminator and a sub-band discriminator. Also, we utilize a pseudo quadrature mirror filter bank to obtain downsampled multi-band speech waveforms while avoiding aliasing. Both objective and subjective experimental results show that Avocodo outperforms baseline GAN-based vocoders. Due to the method for suppressing artifacts, Avocodo can reproduce better quality speech for both single and unseen datasets.
